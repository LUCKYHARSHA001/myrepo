UX Researcher

A UX Researcher plays a critical role in product development by ensuring that design decisions are backed by real user insights. They use a variety of research methods to observe behaviors, gather feedback, and translate findings into actionable guidance for designers and product teams.

Learn more >

  1. Introduction to UX Learn what UX really means — it’s more than just UI. Understand the difference between UX and UI and how UX research fits into the product lifecycle.
  2. Research Methods Dive into core research techniques like interviews, field studies, usability testing, diary studies, and contextual inquiries. Learn when to use each method and how to extract unbiased insights.
  3. User Personas Transform raw research data into meaningful user personas. Learn how to segment users based on behaviors, needs, and goals, helping your team focus on real people instead of assumptions.
  4. Journey Mapping Map out the entire user experience from start to finish. Identify emotions, friction points, and opportunities for improvement in every touchpoint of the user's interaction with the product.
  5. Usability Testing Observe real users interact with your product. Learn how to set goals, create test plans, recruit participants, analyze results, and present your findings to stakeholders effectively.
  6. Quantitative Research Combine surveys, web analytics, heatmaps, and A/B testing to validate hypotheses with numbers. Learn how to use tools like Google Analytics, Mixpanel, and survey platforms.
  7. Accessibility Learn WCAG standards, screen reader testing, and how to advocate for inclusive experiences in your team.
  8. Portfolio & Case Studies Document your research journey in a clear, compelling way. Learn how to write case studies that highlight your process, methods, findings, and impact — not just the final outcomes.
