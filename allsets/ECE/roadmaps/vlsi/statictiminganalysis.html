Static Timing Analysis (STA) Engineer

An STA Engineer specializes in analyzing the timing of digital circuits to ensure they meet performance requirements. They use EDA tools to check setup, hold, and clock constraints, debug timing violations, and collaborate with design and physical teams for timing closure.
A typical day involves running timing analysis, interpreting reports, fixing violations, and optimizing design paths.

Learn more >

  1. Digital Logic Fundamentals Understand the basics: logic gates, flip-flops, and sequential circuits as the foundation for timing analysis.
  2. Timing Concepts Learn setup time, hold time, clock skew, jitter, and how they affect circuit performance.
  3. STA Tool Flows Work with industry-standard STA tools to run timing checks and generate reports.
  4. Timing Paths & Constraints Define and analyze timing paths, set constraints, and understand multi-clock domain analysis.
  5. Timing Violations Debug Debug setup/hold violations, analyze root causes, and work with design teams to fix issues.
  6. Timing Optimization Techniques to optimize critical paths, improve slack, and achieve timing closure.
  7. Power & Signal Integrity Analyze the impact of power and signal integrity on timing and overall chip performance.
  8. Physical Design Interaction Collaborate with PD teams to resolve timing issues related to placement, routing, and clock tree synthesis.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, TCL, or Perl to automate STA flows, parse reports, and manage timing data.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your STA scripts and flows efficiently.
