Physical Design (PD) Engineer

A Physical Design Engineer takes digital designs and implements them at the silicon level, ensuring optimal performance, power, and area. They work with EDA tools to perform synthesis, placement, routing, timing analysis, and signoff checks.
A typical day involves running tool flows, analyzing timing and power reports, debugging layout issues, and collaborating with design and verification teams.

Learn more >

  1. Digital Logic Fundamentals Understand the basics: logic gates, flip-flops, and sequential circuits as the foundation for physical implementation.
  2. RTL to GDSII Flow Learn the complete flow from RTL code to final chip layout (GDSII), including synthesis, placement, and routing.
  3. Logic Synthesis Convert RTL code into gate-level netlists using synthesis tools and understand optimization techniques.
  4. Floorplanning & Placement Plan the chip layout, place standard cells, macros, and optimize for area and performance.
  5. Clock Tree Synthesis (CTS) Design and implement clock trees to ensure proper timing and minimal skew across the chip.
  6. Routing Connect all cells and macros using routing tools, ensuring signal integrity and manufacturability.
  7. Physical Verification Run DRC (Design Rule Check), LVS (Layout vs. Schematic), and other signoff checks to ensure correctness.
  8. Timing, Power & Signal Integrity Analysis Analyze timing, power, and signal integrity to meet chip specifications.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, TCL, or Perl to automate PD flows, generate reports, and manage design data.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your PD scripts and flows efficiently.
