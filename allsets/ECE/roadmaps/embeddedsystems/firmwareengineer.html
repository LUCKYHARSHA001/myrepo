Firmware Engineer

A Firmware Engineer develops low-level software that interacts directly with hardware, enabling devices to function as intended. They write, debug, and optimize code for microcontrollers, SoCs, and embedded systems.
A typical day involves coding in C/C++, debugging hardware interfaces, optimizing performance, and collaborating with hardware and software teams.

  4. Peripheral Interfaces Work with UART, SPI, I2C, GPIO, ADC, and other hardware interfaces.

  8. Low Power & Optimization Techniques for optimizing firmware for power, speed, and memory usage.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, Bash, or TCL to automate build, test, and deployment flows.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your firmware codebase efficiently.
