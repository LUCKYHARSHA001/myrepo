Audio Signal Processing Engineer

An Audio Signal Processing Engineer designs, implements, and optimizes algorithms for processing audio signals in applications such as music, speech, hearing aids, and communications. They work with filters, transforms, effects, and embedded platforms to achieve high-quality audio.
A typical day involves algorithm development, simulation, coding, testing, and collaboration with hardware, software, and product teams.

Learn more >

  1. Signals & Systems Fundamentals Understand discrete-time signals, systems, and basic audio DSP concepts.
  2. Digital Filters & Transforms Design and implement FIR/IIR filters, FFT, DCT, and other transforms for audio applications.
  3. Audio Effects & Processing Develop algorithms for equalization, compression, reverb, echo, and other audio effects.
  4. Speech & Music Processing Implement algorithms for speech enhancement, recognition, synthesis, and music analysis.
  5. Simulation & Modeling Use MATLAB, Python, or Simulink to simulate and model audio processing systems.
  6. Embedded Audio Implementation Implement audio algorithms on microcontrollers, DSP chips, and FPGAs.
  7. Real-Time Audio Processing Optimize algorithms for real-time performance and low latency in audio systems.
  8. Testing & Validation Test and validate audio systems using test signals, measurement tools, and hardware-in-the-loop.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, MATLAB, or Bash to automate audio DSP workflows and data analysis.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your audio DSP codebase efficiently.
