RF (Radio Frequency) Engineer

An RF Engineer designs, tests, and optimizes circuits and systems that operate at radio frequencies, enabling wireless communication. They work with antennas, filters, amplifiers, and wireless protocols for applications in telecom, IoT, and radar.
A typical day involves circuit design, simulation, measurement, troubleshooting, and collaboration with hardware and system teams.

Learn more >

  1. Electromagnetics & RF Fundamentals Understand electromagnetic theory, transmission lines, and RF basics.
  2. RF Circuit Design Design and analyze RF circuits: amplifiers, mixers, oscillators, and filters.
  3. Simulation & Modeling Use simulation tools (ADS, HFSS, SPICE) to model and optimize RF designs.
  4. Antenna Design & Analysis Design, simulate, and test antennas for various wireless applications.
  5. RF Measurement & Testing Use network analyzers, spectrum analyzers, and oscilloscopes for RF measurements.
  6. Wireless Communication Protocols Understand and implement protocols (LTE, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Zigbee, etc.).
  7. RF PCB Layout & Design Design PCBs for RF circuits, focusing on signal integrity and layout best practices.
  8. EMC & Regulatory Compliance Ensure designs meet electromagnetic compatibility and regulatory standards.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, MATLAB, or TCL to automate measurements, data analysis, and simulations.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your RF design files efficiently.
