Aerodynamics Engineer

An Aerodynamics Engineer specializes in the study of airflow around objects, optimizing designs for minimal drag, lift, and stability. They use simulation tools, wind tunnel testing, and analytical methods to improve performance in aerospace, automotive, and energy sectors.
A typical day involves running CFD simulations, analyzing wind tunnel data, collaborating with design teams, and troubleshooting aerodynamic issues.
