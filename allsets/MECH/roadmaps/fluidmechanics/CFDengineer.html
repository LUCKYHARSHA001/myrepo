CFD Engineer

A CFD Engineer specializes in the use of computational fluid dynamics software to simulate, analyze, and optimize fluid flow, heat transfer, and related phenomena. They work in aerospace, automotive, energy, and process industries.
A typical day involves setting up simulations, interpreting results, validating models, and collaborating with design teams.

Learn more >

  1. CFD Fundamentals Master principles of fluid mechanics, numerical methods, and simulation setup.
  2. Mathematical Modeling Apply governing equations (Navier-Stokes, continuity, energy) and discretization techniques.
  3. CFD Software & Tools Use ANSYS Fluent, OpenFOAM, STAR-CCM+, or similar for simulation and analysis.
  4. Meshing & Preprocessing Generate computational grids and preprocess geometry for accurate simulations.
  5. Solver Setup & Postprocessing Configure solvers, boundary conditions, and analyze results.
  6. Validation & Verification Validate models with experimental data and verify simulation accuracy.
  7. Thermodynamics & Heat Transfer Integrate heat transfer and thermodynamics in CFD simulations.
  8. Industry Applications Apply CFD in aerospace, automotive, energy, and process industries.
  9. Programming & Scripting Use Python, MATLAB, or C++ for automation, custom solvers, and data analysis.
  10. Collaboration & Documentation Communicate findings, write technical reports, and collaborate with teams.
