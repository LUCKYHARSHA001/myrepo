Automotive Design Engineer

An Automotive Design Engineer specializes in the design, modeling, and development of vehicles and automotive components. They work on exterior and interior styling, ergonomics, safety, and manufacturability for cars, trucks, and other vehicles.
A typical day involves creating CAD models, collaborating with cross-functional teams, and optimizing designs for performance and aesthetics.

Learn more >

  1. Automotive Fundamentals Master vehicle architecture, systems, and design principles.
  2. CAD Modeling & Drafting Create and modify 2D/3D models using software like CATIA, SolidWorks, or AutoCAD.
  3. Vehicle Styling & Ergonomics Design exterior and interior features for aesthetics and user comfort.
  4. Materials & Manufacturing Select materials and design for manufacturability and cost efficiency.
  5. Safety & Regulations Ensure compliance with safety standards and automotive regulations.
  6. Simulation & Analysis Use FEA, CFD, and other tools to analyze and optimize designs.
  7. Prototyping & Testing Build prototypes and conduct tests for validation and refinement.
  8. Project Management & Collaboration Work with teams, manage timelines, and communicate design intent.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, MATLAB, or Excel to automate design tasks and reporting.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your models and scripts efficiently.
