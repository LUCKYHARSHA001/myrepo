Water Treatment Engineer

A Water Treatment Engineer plans, designs, and manages systems for treating potable water and wastewater. They ensure water quality, regulatory compliance, and environmental protection through effective treatment processes.
A typical day involves process design, plant operation, water quality analysis, troubleshooting, and collaboration with agencies and communities.

Learn more >

  1. Water Treatment Fundamentals Learn about water contaminants, treatment objectives, and regulatory standards.
  2. Process Design Design physical, chemical, and biological treatment processes for water and wastewater.
  3. Plant Operation & Maintenance Operate and maintain water treatment plants and equipment.
  4. Water Quality Analysis Monitor and analyze water quality parameters and compliance.
  5. Sludge & Residuals Management Manage sludge, biosolids, and residuals from treatment processes.
  6. Automation & Control Implement automated control systems for efficient plant operation.
  7. Troubleshooting & Optimization Identify and resolve operational issues, optimize treatment processes.
  8. Environmental Impact Assessment Assess and mitigate environmental impacts of treatment plants.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage water treatment projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, communities, and stakeholders for project delivery.
