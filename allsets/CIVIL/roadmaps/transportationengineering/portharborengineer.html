Port & Harbor Engineer

A Port & Harbor Engineer plans, designs, and manages the construction and maintenance of ports, harbors, docks, and marine terminals. They ensure safe, efficient, and sustainable maritime operations.
A typical day involves site planning, structural design, dredging, environmental assessment, and collaboration with agencies and contractors.

Learn more >

  1. Port & Harbor Engineering Fundamentals Learn about port types, functions, and design principles.
  2. Site Planning & Layout Plan port and harbor site layout, zoning, and land use for optimal operations.
  3. Structural Design Design docks, wharves, piers, breakwaters, and marine terminals.
  4. Dredging & Waterway Management Plan and manage dredging operations and waterway maintenance.
  5. Marine Infrastructure Design and maintain marine infrastructure for cargo and passenger operations.
  6. Environmental Impact Assessment Assess and mitigate environmental impacts of port projects.
  7. Construction Management Plan, supervise, and manage port and harbor construction activities.
  8. Operations & Maintenance Manage port operations, maintenance, and safety procedures.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage port and harbor projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, shipping companies, and stakeholders for project delivery.
