Remote Sensing Specialist

A Remote Sensing Specialist acquires, processes, and analyzes data from satellites, aircraft, and drones to support environmental, infrastructure, and planning projects. They use advanced remote sensing technologies and image analysis techniques.
A typical day involves data acquisition, image processing, interpretation, and collaboration with geospatial teams.

Learn more >

  1. Remote Sensing Fundamentals Learn about remote sensing principles, platforms, and sensors.
  2. Data Acquisition & Preprocessing Collect and preprocess satellite, aerial, and drone imagery.
  3. Image Processing & Enhancement Apply image correction, enhancement, and classification techniques.
  4. GIS Integration Integrate remote sensing data with GIS for spatial analysis.
  5. Change Detection & Monitoring Monitor land use, environmental changes, and infrastructure using remote sensing.
  6. Advanced Analysis & Modeling Perform advanced spatial analysis and modeling using remote sensing data.
  7. Database Management Manage remote sensing databases and metadata.
  8. Field Validation Validate remote sensing data accuracy through ground truthing and surveys.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage remote sensing projects and deliverables.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with geospatial professionals, engineers, and stakeholders for project delivery.
