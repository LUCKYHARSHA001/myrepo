Digital Forensics Analyst

The Digital Forensics Analyst role involves investigating cyber incidents by collecting and analyzing digital evidence. They retrieve data from devices, examine logs, and trace unauthorized activity to support legal or internal actions. Their work demands precision, legal awareness, and a strong grasp of forensic tools.

Learn more >

  1. AutopsyAnalyzes hard drives and mobile devices to recover files, view metadata, and investigate deleted content.
  2. FTK (Forensic Toolkit)Processes data sets for digital investigations and reveals hidden or encrypted files on storage media.
  3. EnCasePerforms in-depth file system analysis to uncover digital evidence for legal investigations.
  4. VolatilityExtracts artifacts from memory dumps to detect malware, process info, and runtime activity.
  5. X-Ways ForensicsProvides powerful disk imaging, data carving, and registry analysis for forensic experts.
