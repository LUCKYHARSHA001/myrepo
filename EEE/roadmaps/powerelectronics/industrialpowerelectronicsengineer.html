Industrial Power Electronics Engineer

Industrial Power Electronics Engineers design, implement, and maintain power electronic systems for industrial automation, manufacturing, and energy conversion. They focus on efficiency, reliability, and innovation in industrial environments.
A typical day involves system design, programming, troubleshooting, and collaboration with manufacturing and energy teams.

Learn more >

  1. Electrical & Electronics Fundamentals Build a strong foundation in electrical circuits, semiconductors, and materials.
  2. Industrial Power Electronic Devices Learn about industrial-grade diodes, transistors, thyristors, IGBTs, and MOSFETs.
  3. Converter & Inverter Systems Study topologies, design, and control of converters and inverters for industrial use.
  4. Motor Drives & Control Design and control of motor drives for industrial automation.
  5. Simulation & Modeling Use simulation tools (MATLAB, PSpice) for design and analysis.
  6. Thermal Management & Protection Design cooling systems and protection schemes for industrial power electronics.
  7. Testing & Commissioning Test, commission, and troubleshoot industrial power electronic systems.
  8. Integration & Application Integrate power electronics in industrial automation and manufacturing systems.
  9. Safety & Standards Work with safety standards (IEC, IEEE) and ensure safe operation of industrial power electronics.
  10. Documentation & Reporting Document designs, maintenance, and process improvements.
  11. Soft Skills Communication, teamwork, and project management for successful delivery.
