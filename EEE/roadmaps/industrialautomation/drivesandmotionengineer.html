Drives & Motion Engineer

Drives & Motion Engineers design, implement, and maintain drive systems and motion control solutions for industrial automation and manufacturing. They focus on reliability, efficiency, and safety in automated environments.
A typical day involves system design, programming, troubleshooting, and collaboration with manufacturing and process teams.

Learn more >

  1. Mathematics & Control Theory Build a strong foundation in mathematics, control theory, and system dynamics.
  2. Drive Systems Fundamentals Learn about drive principles, types, and industrial protocols.
  3. Motion Control Programming Study programming languages and frameworks for motion control (PLC, C++, Python).
  4. Automation & Integration Integrate drive and motion solutions with automation systems and commission them for operation.
  5. Control Strategies Explore PID, advanced control algorithms, and real-time control systems for drives and motion.
  6. Machine Vision & AI Learn about machine vision, sensors, and AI for motion control applications.
  7. Safety & Standards Work with safety standards (ISO, IEC) and ensure safe operation of drive and motion systems.
  8. Troubleshooting & Maintenance Troubleshoot and maintain drive and motion systems.
  9. Documentation & Reporting Document drive and motion designs, maintenance, and process improvements.
  10. Soft Skills Communication, teamwork, and project management for successful delivery.
