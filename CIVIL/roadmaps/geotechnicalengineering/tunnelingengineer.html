Tunneling Engineer

A Tunneling Engineer specializes in the planning, design, and construction of tunnels for transportation, utilities, and infrastructure. They address geological, geotechnical, and safety challenges to ensure successful tunnel delivery.
A typical day involves site investigation, tunnel alignment design, excavation method selection, ground support, monitoring, and collaboration with multidisciplinary teams.

Learn more >

  1. Tunnel Engineering Fundamentals Learn about tunnel types, history, and basic principles.
  2. Geological & Geotechnical Investigation Conduct site investigations, rock/soil classification, and risk assessment.
  3. Tunnel Alignment & Design Plan tunnel routes, cross-sections, and design parameters.
  4. Excavation Methods Select and apply excavation techniques (TBM, NATM, drill & blast, cut & cover).
  5. Ground Support & Lining Design and install supports (shotcrete, rock bolts, segmental lining).
  6. Waterproofing & Drainage Implement waterproofing systems and drainage solutions for tunnels.
  7. Instrumentation & Monitoring Monitor ground movement, tunnel stability, and safety during construction.
  8. Construction Supervision Oversee tunnel construction, quality control, and safety management.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage tunneling projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with engineers, geologists, contractors, and stakeholders for project delivery.
