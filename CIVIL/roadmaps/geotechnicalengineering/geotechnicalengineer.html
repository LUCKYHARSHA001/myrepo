Geotechnical Engineer

A Geotechnical Engineer investigates soil and rock properties, analyzes ground conditions, and designs foundations, retaining structures, and earthworks. They ensure stability, safety, and performance of civil engineering projects.
A typical day involves site investigation, laboratory testing, geotechnical analysis, design, and collaboration with project teams.

Learn more >

  1. Geotechnical Engineering Fundamentals Learn about soil mechanics, rock mechanics, and ground behavior.
  2. Site Investigation & Sampling Plan and conduct site investigations, drilling, and sampling.
  3. Laboratory Testing Perform soil and rock tests to determine engineering properties.
  4. Geotechnical Analysis Analyze bearing capacity, settlement, slope stability, and earth pressures.
  5. Foundation Design Design shallow and deep foundations, retaining walls, and ground improvement.
  6. Earthworks & Excavation Plan and supervise earthworks, excavation, and embankment construction.
  7. Groundwater & Seepage Analysis Assess groundwater conditions and design dewatering systems.
  8. Geotechnical Instrumentation Install and monitor instruments for ground movement and stability.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage geotechnical projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with engineers, contractors, and stakeholders for project delivery.
