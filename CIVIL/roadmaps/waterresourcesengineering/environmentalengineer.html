Environmental Engineer

An Environmental Engineer develops solutions to environmental problems, including water and air pollution, waste management, and sustainability. They ensure compliance with regulations and promote public health and environmental protection.
A typical day involves environmental assessment, design of treatment systems, fieldwork, regulatory compliance, and collaboration with agencies and communities.

Learn more >

  1. Environmental Engineering Fundamentals Learn about environmental systems, pollution sources, and regulations.
  2. Water & Wastewater Treatment Design and manage systems for water purification and wastewater treatment.
  3. Air Quality Management Monitor and control air pollution, emissions, and indoor air quality.
  4. Solid & Hazardous Waste Management Plan and implement waste collection, recycling, and disposal systems.
  5. Environmental Impact Assessment Assess and mitigate environmental impacts of projects and activities.
  6. GIS & Remote Sensing Use GIS and remote sensing for environmental monitoring and analysis.
  7. Regulatory Compliance Ensure compliance with environmental laws, permits, and standards.
  8. Fieldwork & Data Collection Conduct site surveys, collect samples, and monitor environmental parameters.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage environmental projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, communities, and stakeholders for project delivery.
