Water Resources Engineer

A Water Resources Engineer plans, designs, and manages systems for water supply, distribution, flood control, irrigation, and wastewater treatment. They ensure sustainable and efficient use of water resources for communities and industries.
A typical day involves hydraulic modeling, infrastructure design, project management, fieldwork, and collaboration with stakeholders.

Learn more >

  1. Water Resources Fundamentals Learn about hydrology, hydraulics, and water resource systems.
  2. Hydraulic Modeling Use software (EPANET, HEC-RAS, SWMM) for modeling water flow and distribution.
  3. Water Supply & Distribution Design systems for potable water supply, storage, and distribution networks.
  4. Flood Control & Management Plan and design flood control structures, levees, and drainage systems.
  5. Irrigation Systems Design and manage irrigation networks for agriculture and landscaping.
  6. Wastewater Treatment Plan and design wastewater collection, treatment, and disposal systems.
  7. Environmental Impact Assessment Assess and mitigate environmental impacts of water projects.
  8. Fieldwork & Data Collection Conduct site surveys, collect data, and monitor water systems.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage water resources projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with engineers, agencies, and stakeholders for project delivery.
