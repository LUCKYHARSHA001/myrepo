Hydrologist

A Hydrologist studies the movement, distribution, and quality of water in the Earth's atmosphere and surface. They analyze rainfall, runoff, groundwater, and water quality to support water resource management, flood control, and environmental protection.
A typical day involves data collection, hydrological modeling, fieldwork, analysis, and collaboration with engineers and environmental scientists.

Learn more >

  1. Hydrology Fundamentals Learn about the hydrologic cycle, precipitation, evaporation, and infiltration.
  2. Data Collection & Analysis Collect and analyze rainfall, streamflow, and groundwater data.
  3. Hydrological Modeling Use models (HEC-HMS, SWAT, MODFLOW) to simulate water movement and distribution.
  4. Water Quality Analysis Assess water quality parameters and impacts on ecosystems and human health.
  5. Flood & Drought Assessment Analyze flood risks, drought impacts, and develop mitigation strategies.
  6. Groundwater Studies Investigate aquifers, groundwater flow, and recharge processes.
  7. GIS & Remote Sensing Use GIS and remote sensing tools for hydrological mapping and analysis.
  8. Fieldwork & Instrumentation Conduct field surveys, install instruments, and collect water samples.
  9. Reporting & Communication Prepare technical reports and communicate findings to stakeholders.
  10. Collaboration & Research Work with engineers, scientists, and participate in research projects.
