Waste Management Engineer

A Waste Management Engineer plans, designs, and manages systems for solid and hazardous waste collection, treatment, recycling, and disposal. They ensure environmental protection, regulatory compliance, and sustainability.
A typical day involves system design, facility operation, waste audits, environmental assessment, and collaboration with agencies and communities.

Learn more >

  1. Waste Management Fundamentals Learn about waste types, sources, and management principles.
  2. Collection & Transportation Design and manage systems for waste collection and transportation.
  3. Treatment & Processing Plan and implement physical, chemical, and biological treatment processes.
  4. Recycling & Resource Recovery Promote recycling, composting, and resource recovery initiatives.
  5. Hazardous Waste Management Manage hazardous waste, including storage, treatment, and disposal.
  6. Facility Design & Operation Design and operate landfills, incinerators, and transfer stations.
  7. Environmental Impact Assessment Assess and mitigate environmental impacts of waste management projects.
  8. Regulatory Compliance Ensure compliance with waste management laws, permits, and standards.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage waste management projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, communities, and stakeholders for project delivery.
