Air Quality Engineer

An Air Quality Engineer monitors, analyzes, and controls air pollution from industrial, urban, and natural sources. They design systems to improve air quality, ensure regulatory compliance, and protect public health.
A typical day involves air quality monitoring, emissions analysis, system design, regulatory compliance, and collaboration with agencies and communities.

Learn more >

  1. Air Quality Fundamentals Learn about air pollutants, sources, and atmospheric processes.
  2. Air Quality Monitoring Design and operate air quality monitoring networks and instruments.
  3. Emissions Analysis Analyze emissions from vehicles, industries, and natural sources.
  4. Control Technologies Design and implement air pollution control systems and technologies.
  5. Indoor Air Quality Assess and improve air quality in buildings and indoor environments.
  6. Modeling & Simulation Use models to predict air pollution dispersion and impacts.
  7. Regulatory Compliance Ensure compliance with air quality standards and regulations.
  8. Fieldwork & Data Collection Conduct site surveys, collect samples, and monitor air quality parameters.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage air quality projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, communities, and stakeholders for project delivery.
