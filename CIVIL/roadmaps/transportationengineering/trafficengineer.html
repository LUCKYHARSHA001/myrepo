Traffic Engineer

A Traffic Engineer plans, designs, and manages traffic flow, control systems, and safety measures for roads and intersections. They optimize transportation efficiency and safety for vehicles, pedestrians, and cyclists.
A typical day involves traffic studies, signal design, safety analysis, site supervision, and collaboration with agencies and communities.

Learn more >

  1. Traffic Engineering Fundamentals Learn about traffic flow theory, control devices, and design principles.
  2. Traffic Studies & Data Collection Conduct traffic counts, surveys, and analyze travel patterns.
  3. Signal Design & Control Design traffic signals, timing plans, and control systems for intersections.
  4. Road Safety Analysis Analyze accident data, design safety improvements, and implement measures.
  5. Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) Implement ITS technologies for traffic management and information.
  6. Pedestrian & Bicycle Facilities Design safe and accessible facilities for non-motorized users.
  7. Parking & Access Management Plan and manage parking facilities and access points.
  8. Construction Management Plan, supervise, and manage traffic-related construction activities.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage traffic engineering projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, communities, and stakeholders for project delivery.
