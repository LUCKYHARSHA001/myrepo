Highway Engineer

A Highway Engineer plans, designs, and manages the construction and maintenance of highways, roads, and expressways. They ensure safety, efficiency, and sustainability of transportation networks.
A typical day involves geometric design, pavement analysis, traffic studies, site supervision, and collaboration with agencies and contractors.

Learn more >

  1. Highway Engineering Fundamentals Learn about road types, functions, and design principles.
  2. Geometric Design Design road alignments, profiles, cross-sections, and intersections.
  3. Pavement Design & Materials Select and design pavement structures using appropriate materials.
  4. Traffic Engineering Conduct traffic studies, signal design, and safety analysis.
  5. Drainage & Environmental Considerations Design drainage systems and assess environmental impacts.
  6. Construction Management Plan, supervise, and manage highway construction activities.
  7. Maintenance & Rehabilitation Inspect, maintain, and rehabilitate road infrastructure.
  8. GIS & Remote Sensing Use GIS and remote sensing for highway planning and monitoring.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage highway projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, communities, and stakeholders for project delivery.
