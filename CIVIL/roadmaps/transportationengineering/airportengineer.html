Airport Engineer

An Airport Engineer plans, designs, and manages the construction and maintenance of airport runways, taxiways, terminals, and support facilities. They ensure safety, efficiency, and compliance with aviation standards.
A typical day involves site planning, pavement design, airside/landside operations, safety analysis, and collaboration with agencies and contractors.

Learn more >

  1. Airport Engineering Fundamentals Learn about airport types, functions, and design principles.
  2. Site Planning & Layout Plan airport site layout, zoning, and land use for optimal operations.
  3. Pavement Design & Materials Design runways, taxiways, and aprons using appropriate materials.
  4. Terminal & Facility Design Plan and design passenger terminals, cargo facilities, and support buildings.
  5. Airside & Landside Operations Manage airside (runways, taxiways) and landside (terminals, parking) operations.
  6. Safety & Security Management Ensure compliance with aviation safety and security regulations.
  7. Environmental Impact Assessment Assess and mitigate environmental impacts of airport projects.
  8. Construction Management Plan, supervise, and manage airport construction activities.
  9. Project Management Plan, schedule, and manage airport projects and budgets.
  10. Collaboration & Communication Work with agencies, airlines, and stakeholders for project delivery.
