Digital Signal Processing (DSP) Engineer

A DSP Engineer designs, implements, and optimizes algorithms for processing digital signals in applications such as audio, communications, radar, and biomedical devices. They work with filters, transforms, and embedded platforms to achieve real-time performance.
A typical day involves algorithm development, simulation, coding, testing, and collaboration with hardware and software teams.

Learn more >

  1. Signals & Systems Fundamentals Understand discrete-time signals, systems, and basic DSP concepts.
  2. Digital Filters & Transforms Design and implement FIR/IIR filters, FFT, DCT, and other transforms.
  3. DSP Algorithms & Applications Develop algorithms for audio, communications, image, and biomedical signal processing.
  4. Simulation & Modeling Use MATLAB, Python, or Simulink to simulate and model DSP systems.
  5. Embedded DSP Implementation Implement DSP algorithms on microcontrollers, DSP chips, and FPGAs.
  6. Real-Time Processing Optimize algorithms for real-time performance and low latency.
  7. Testing & Validation Test and validate DSP systems using test signals, measurement tools, and hardware-in-the-loop.
  8. Signal Integrity & Noise Analysis Analyze and mitigate noise, distortion, and signal integrity issues.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, MATLAB, or Bash to automate DSP workflows and data analysis.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your DSP codebase efficiently.
