Wireless Network Engineer

A Wireless Network Engineer designs, implements, and maintains wireless networks (Wi-Fi, cellular, IoT, etc.), ensuring coverage, performance, and security. They work with RF, antennas, protocols, and network devices for enterprise and service provider environments.
A typical day involves site surveys, network design, troubleshooting, monitoring, and collaborating with IT and RF teams.

Learn more >

  1. Wireless Fundamentals Understand RF basics, propagation, modulation, and wireless standards (IEEE 802.11, 3G/4G/5G).
  2. Wireless Network Design Plan and design wireless networks for coverage, capacity, and performance.
  3. RF & Antenna Concepts Work with antennas, RF propagation, and site surveys for optimal deployment.
  4. Wireless Protocols & Security Implement and secure wireless protocols (WPA2, WPA3, LTE, BLE, Zigbee, etc.).
  5. Network Devices & Controllers Configure and manage access points, controllers, and wireless gateways.
  6. Wireless Monitoring & Troubleshooting Use tools (Ekahau, AirMagnet, Wireshark) to monitor and troubleshoot wireless networks.
  7. Cellular & IoT Networks Work with cellular (3G/4G/5G) and IoT wireless technologies for modern applications.
  8. Performance & Optimization Optimize wireless networks for speed, reliability, and user experience.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, Bash, or TCL to automate wireless network configuration and management.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your wireless network scripts and documentation efficiently.
