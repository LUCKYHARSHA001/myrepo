Cabling / Infrastructure Technician

A Cabling / Infrastructure Technician installs, maintains, and troubleshoots network cabling, racks, and related infrastructure for data centers, offices, and industrial environments. They ensure reliable connectivity and support for network devices.
A typical day involves cable installation, termination, testing, documentation, and collaborating with network and IT teams.

Learn more >

  1. Networking & Cabling Basics Understand basic networking concepts, cable types (Ethernet, fiber), and standards (TIA/EIA).
  2. Cable Installation & Routing Learn best practices for cable installation, routing, and management in various environments.
  3. Cable Termination & Testing Terminate cables (RJ45, fiber connectors), test for continuity, and certify installations.
  4. Rack & Infrastructure Setup Install racks, patch panels, and organize network equipment for accessibility and airflow.
  5. Documentation & Labeling Document cable runs, label connections, and maintain accurate infrastructure records.
  6. Troubleshooting & Repair Diagnose and repair cabling issues using testers, tone generators, and other tools.
  7. Safety & Compliance Follow safety protocols and comply with industry standards and regulations.
  8. Wireless Infrastructure Install and maintain wireless access points and related cabling.
  9. Tools & Equipment Use crimpers, punch-down tools, fiber cleavers, and testers for cabling work.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your infrastructure documentation efficiently.
