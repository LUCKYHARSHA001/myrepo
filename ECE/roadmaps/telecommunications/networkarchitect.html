Network Architect

A Network Architect designs and plans complex network infrastructures for enterprises, data centers, and service providers. They define network requirements, select technologies, and ensure scalability, security, and reliability.
A typical day involves architecture design, technology evaluation, documentation, and collaboration with engineering and business teams.

Learn more >

  1. Networking Fundamentals Master OSI model, TCP/IP, Ethernet, and foundational networking concepts.
  2. Network Design & Topologies Design network topologies for scalability, redundancy, and performance.
  3. Enterprise & Data Center Networking Architect large-scale networks for enterprises and data centers, including core, distribution, and access layers.
  4. Routing & Switching Architectures Design and optimize routing and switching architectures (OSPF, BGP, MPLS, VLANs).
  5. Network Security Architecture Plan and implement security architectures (firewalls, VPNs, IDS/IPS, segmentation).
  6. Cloud & Hybrid Networking Design cloud, hybrid, and multi-cloud network architectures (AWS, Azure, GCP).
  7. Wireless & Mobile Architectures Architect Wi-Fi, cellular, and mobile networks for coverage and performance.
  8. Network Automation & Orchestration Use SDN, NFV, and automation tools to manage and optimize network infrastructures.
  9. Documentation & Standards Document architectures, create diagrams, and ensure compliance with industry standards.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your architecture documents and scripts efficiently.
