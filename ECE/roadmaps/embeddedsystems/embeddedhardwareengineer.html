Embedded Hardware Engineer

An Embedded Hardware Engineer designs and develops the physical components of embedded systems, including PCBs, microcontrollers, sensors, and interfaces. They work with circuit design, prototyping, and testing to ensure reliable hardware operation.
A typical day involves schematic capture, PCB layout, hardware debugging, and collaborating with firmware and software teams.

Learn more >

  1. Electronics Fundamentals Understand basic electronics, circuit theory, and semiconductor devices.
  2. Embedded Systems Basics Learn about microcontrollers, SoCs, and hardware architectures for embedded systems.
  3. Schematic Design Use EDA tools to create circuit schematics for embedded hardware.
  4. PCB Layout & Design Design printed circuit boards, select components, and optimize for manufacturability.
  5. Signal Integrity & EMC Analyze and mitigate signal integrity and electromagnetic compatibility issues.
  6. Prototyping & Testing Build hardware prototypes, perform functional testing, and debug hardware issues.
  7. Power Management Design and analyze power supplies, regulators, and low-power techniques for embedded hardware.
  8. Sensor & Interface Integration Integrate sensors, displays, and communication interfaces (UART, SPI, I2C, etc.).
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, Bash, or TCL to automate hardware testing and data collection.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your hardware design files efficiently.
