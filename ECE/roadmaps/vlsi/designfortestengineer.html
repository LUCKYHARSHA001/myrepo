Design for Test (DFT) Engineer

A DFT Engineer specializes in making digital designs testable, ensuring that chips can be efficiently tested for manufacturing defects. They implement test structures, develop test strategies, and work closely with design and test teams to maximize product quality and yield.
A typical day involves inserting scan chains, writing test patterns, analyzing coverage, and debugging test failures.

Learn more >

  1. Digital Logic Fundamentals Understand the basics: logic gates, flip-flops, and sequential circuits as the foundation for test structures.
  2. DFT Concepts & Techniques Learn scan design, boundary scan, BIST (Built-In Self-Test), and other core DFT methodologies.
  3. Scan Insertion & ATPG Insert scan chains and use Automatic Test Pattern Generation tools to create effective test patterns.
  4. Fault Models & Coverage Study stuck-at, transition, and other fault models. Analyze and improve fault coverage.
  5. Test Pattern Simulation Simulate test patterns to validate their effectiveness and debug failures.
  6. DFT Tool Flows Work with industry-standard DFT tools for scan insertion, ATPG, and coverage analysis.
  7. Low Power DFT Learn techniques for testing low-power designs, including scan compression and power-aware testing.
  8. Yield Analysis & Debug Analyze test results, debug yield issues, and work with failure analysis teams.
  9. Scripting & Automation Use Python, TCL, or Perl to automate DFT flows, generate reports, and manage test data.
  10. Version Control (Git) Track changes, collaborate with teams, and manage your DFT scripts and flows efficiently.
