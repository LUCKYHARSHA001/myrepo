Hardware Engineer (Robotics)

Hardware Engineers in robotics design, develop, and test the physical components of robots, including sensors, actuators, PCBs, and embedded systems. They work closely with software and mechanical teams to ensure robust and reliable hardware integration.
A typical day involves circuit design, prototyping, testing, and troubleshooting.

  1. Mathematics & Physics Foundations Build a strong base in mathematics and physics for hardware design.
  2. Electronics Fundamentals Learn circuit theory, analog/digital electronics, and PCB design.
  3. Embedded Systems Work with microcontrollers, sensors, and actuators for robot hardware.
  4. PCB Design & Prototyping Design and prototype printed circuit boards for robotics applications.
  5. Power Systems & Battery Management Understand power electronics and battery management for mobile robots.
  6. Sensor Integration Integrate various sensors (IMU, camera, lidar) with hardware platforms.
  7. Testing & Validation Test and validate hardware designs for reliability and performance.
  8. Manufacturing & Assembly Work with manufacturing processes and assembly for robotics hardware.
  9. Collaboration & Documentation Work with cross-functional teams and document hardware designs.
  10. Soft Skills Communication, teamwork, and project management for successful delivery.
